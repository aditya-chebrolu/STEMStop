Gregor Mendel

Gregor Mendel is known as the “Father of Genetics”
He was a monk that ran several experiments involving pea plants and seeing how the features of the parents were passed onto their offspring


Punnett Squares

Punnett Squares are a form of predicting the likelihood of an offspring having a certain trait depending on the parents
Dominant Traits: These traits will be expressed whenever the organism has it and is denoted with capital letters on the Punnett Square
Recessive Traits: These traits will be only be expressed when an organism doesn’t have a conflicting dominant trait and is denoted with lowercase letters


Multiple Alleles, Incomplete, and Codominance

Multiple Alleles: This is when there are more than 2 genotypes for an organism
Human Blood is A, B, O
Incomplete Dominance: This is when the dominant and recessive traits of a heterozygous trait gets blended together
Red Flower + White Flower =  Pink Flower
Codominance: This is when the dominant and recessive traits of a heterozygous trait both are expressed separately
White Flower + Black Flower = Spotted Flower

Succession

What is Succession?
Succession is the process of life growing back into an environment that was previously uninhabited or destroyed


Primary Succession
Primary Succession is when life grows onto a place that never had living things beforehand
Examples are: Newly exposed rock in a glacier, new rock formed by volcanoes,etc.



Secondary Succession
Secondary Succession occurs when something reduces an already established ecosystem and life regrows and recovers
Life can be wiped out due to fires, floods, hurricanes, etc. 
Biggest difference from primary is that there is still soil in the ecosystem
